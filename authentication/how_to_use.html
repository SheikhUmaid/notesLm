Welcome to NotesLM

NotesLM is a simple, structured, and focused space where you can log in, enter your chat name, create your own notebooks, upload your study files, and ask as many questions as you want about your content. Everything is centered around one idea: keep your learning flow smooth and your notes always accessible.

On this page, we’ll walk through the experience in detail. Even though the idea is brief and clear — login to NotesLM, enter your chat name, create a notebook, upload files, and ask questions — we’re going to explain every step in a calm, long, and descriptive way so you always know exactly what to expect from the platform.

In short: you log in, choose your chat name so NotesLM knows how to talk to you, create a notebook for your subject or topic, upload your files like PDFs, notes, and slides, and then simply start asking questions. NotesLM stays with you while you learn.

Step 1: Login to NotesLM

The very first thing you do is log in to NotesLM. This is your entry point, your front door to everything that will follow. When you arrive at the login page, you are greeted with the NotesLM branding and a simple form that invites you to sign in to your learning space.

Logging in is how NotesLM connects you with your personal notebooks, your uploaded files, and your previous question history. Once you are logged in, everything on the platform becomes personalized around your learning journey. You are no longer just a visitor — you become a learner with a defined workspace.

The login flow is intentionally straightforward. You see fields for your username and password, and once you enter your details and click the button to continue, NotesLM prepares your environment. You don’t have to think about the internal systems or any complicated process. You just know that after logging in, your dashboard, your notebooks, your uploaded materials, and your chat interactions will all be ready for you.

As soon as you log in successfully, NotesLM treats you as an individual learner with your own preferences, your own topics, and your own pace. You are now ready for the next important step: telling NotesLM who you are in the chat.

Step 2: Enter Your Chat Name

After logging in, NotesLM encourages you to enter your chat name. Your chat name is how you want to be addressed when you interact with the system. It’s a simple thing, but it makes the experience feel more personalized and more human.

You might choose your real name, a nickname, or a study alias that you like. Once you enter this chat name, NotesLM can refer to you directly during your sessions, making every response feel like it’s written to you and not just to any generic user. This name also helps when you create notebooks or when your interactions are saved; it anchors everything around your identity as a learner.

The chat name also helps keep different profiles clear in your mind. If you want to use NotesLM differently for school, university, or personal projects, you can choose a chat name that matches your context. No matter how you use it, the main idea is simple: you log in and then you tell NotesLM, “This is how you should talk to me.”

Think of your chat name as your learning identity inside NotesLM. Every notebook, uploaded file, and conversation is tied back to this identity so your learning stays organized.

Step 3: Create a Notebook for Your Learning

Once you have logged in and set your chat name, NotesLM invites you to create your first notebook. A notebook in NotesLM is like a dedicated space for a specific subject, topic, or project. Instead of mixing everything together, you can separate your learning areas cleanly.

For example, you might create one notebook called “Data Structures”, another one called “Operating Systems”, another for “Machine Learning Basics”, or anything else you are currently studying. Each notebook becomes a hub where your files, your questions, and your answers all live together in a structured way.

When you create a notebook, you typically give it a name that describes the topic you care about and sometimes a short description so that future you immediately remembers what this notebook is about. Over time, as you add more notebooks, this structure keeps your NotesLM space neat and easy to navigate.

Inside each notebook, you can add more content, ask focused questions, and treat it as a dedicated environment for one area of study. By separating things into notebooks like this, you avoid clutter and stay mentally organized, which matters a lot when you are learning multiple things in parallel.

Step 4: Upload Your Files into NotesLM

After you create a notebook, the next natural step is to upload your files. NotesLM is built so that you do not have to switch between different apps, scattered folders, and random documents. You bring your files directly into NotesLM and keep them close to your conversations and questions.

These files could be lecture slides, PDF notes, assignment instructions, reference books, project documents, or even images that contain important information. When you upload them into a notebook that you have created, NotesLM understands that these files belong to that specific learning area.

The upload process is simple. You choose the notebook, click on the upload area, select your files from your device, and let NotesLM attach them to your space. Once uploaded, the files become available whenever you need to refer back to them, and they can also act as a base for the questions you ask later.

The idea is that instead of thinking, “Where did I keep that PDF?” you simply think, “I uploaded that PDF into my NotesLM notebook, so I know exactly where it is.” Over time, this reduces friction every time you switch back to studying.

Login → choose your chat name → create a notebook → upload your files. Once these steps are done, you are fully set up and can start asking questions confidently.

Step 5: Ask Questions About Your Notes and Files

With your notebook created and your files uploaded, you now reach one of the most important parts of NotesLM: asking questions. This is where the platform turns into a true learning partner. You don’t just keep your files in one place; you actually interact with them through your questions.

You might have doubts about a specific topic in your notes, or you might want a summary of a long PDF, or you may be trying to understand what a particular concept means in simple terms. NotesLM lets you ask those questions naturally, in your own words, using the chat interface that addresses you by your chosen chat name.

For example, after logging in and opening your “Data Structures” notebook, you might say something like:

  • Question: “Can you explain the difference between a stack and a queue using simple examples?”
  • Question: “Summarize chapter 3 of this PDF I uploaded and highlight the main formulas.”
  • Question: “Give me some practice questions based on these notes for revision.”

NotesLM responds within the context of your notebook and the files you have uploaded. You don’t need to manually reference everything every time. Instead, your conversation stays grounded in your chosen topic, your uploaded content, and your chat identity.

The pattern stays simple but powerful: you log in, you have your chat name, you select or create a notebook, your files are already there, and you keep asking questions whenever something is unclear or whenever you want to deepen your understanding. The platform becomes a quiet companion that stays focused on what you are working on.

Using NotesLM Regularly

The more you use NotesLM, the more natural this flow becomes. Every session starts in roughly the same way: you log in, NotesLM recognizes you, you see your notebooks, and you decide where you want to focus. Over time, your notebooks fill up with files, questions, and answers that represent your unique learning path.

You might start your day by logging in and opening your main notebook for that day’s study plan. You might upload a new set of lecture slides or an assignment sheet your teacher shared. Then you ask a few questions to make sure you fully understand the material. Later, you come back and review the answers, ask follow-up questions, or create new notebooks for future subjects.

This repeating rhythm — login → chat name → notebook → upload → questions — becomes the backbone of how you interact with NotesLM. It is not complicated; it is intentionally repetitive so that you never waste energy trying to remember how to use the platform. Instead, all of your mental energy goes into the actual learning itself.

Quick Summary of the Flow

Even though this page describes the flow in a very detailed and extended way, the core idea behind NotesLM is actually brief and simple:

  • You login to NotesLM using your account.
  • You enter your chat name so the system can address you personally.
  • You create a notebook for each subject or project you care about.
  • You upload your files—PDFs, slides, notes, and more—into those notebooks.
  • You ask questions about your notes, your files, and your topics, and you use the answers to learn better.

These few steps repeat every time you use the platform, and they form the foundation of your learning environment. All the detailed explanation on this page simply exists to make sure each small piece is clear in your mind and that you always know exactly why you are doing what you are doing when you interact with NotesLM.

Whenever you think about NotesLM, remember this simple sentence: Login to NotesLM, enter your chat name, create a notebook, upload your files, and ask questions as you learn.
